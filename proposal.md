# Challenging the Call: MLB's Automated Ball-Strike Revolution

For our final project we will build an interactive explorable explanation of MLB's new Automated Ball-Strike (ABS) Challenge System, which debuted league-wide in the 2026 season. The ABS system uses Hawk-Eye camera technology to let batters and catchers challenge ball-and-strike calls in real time, giving teams three challenges per game to appeal to a machine rather than a human umpire. We will use live data pulled from Baseball Savant's ABS leaderboard and Statcast pitch-by-pitch feed — over 69,000 pitches through mid-May 2026 — to tell the story of how this rule change is reshaping strategy at every level of the game. Our anchor visualization will be a dual strike zone heatmap showing where umpires historically place the zone versus where the ABS system draws it, making the core tension of the project immediately visible. From there, scrollytelling panels will walk the reader through which teams are winning the challenge battle, which individual batters are extracting the most value from the system, and how overturned calls are directly flipping strikeouts into walks and vice versa. The final deliverable will be a fully interactive D3.js web page deployed on GitHub Pages, designed so that a casual baseball fan can understand ABS and its impact within minutes of arriving on the page.
